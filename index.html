How the Community Powers FraudWatch

FraudWatch is not a government database or a corporate product. It is built on community intelligence — real reports from real victims, verified and published so others do not suffer the same loss.

Every record in the database exists because someone chose to report it. Every search that prevents a fraud is a victory for the person who submitted that report — even if they never know who they saved.

The Journey of a Fraud Report

  1. A victim or witness submits a fraud report — name, phone number, evidence, description of what happened.
  2. Our admin team verifies the report — checks for duplicate entries, reviews evidence quality, confirms category.
  3. The verified record is published to the live public registry — instantly searchable by anyone in Nigeria and beyond.
  4. The community shares the record — on WhatsApp, Twitter/X, Instagram — reaching people who might be targeted next.
  5. Future potential victims search the platform before sending money. The fraudster's reach is cut short.

OUR PROMISES TO THE COMMUNITY

These are not aspirations. These are commitments. Hold us to them.

1

FraudWatch Will Always Be Free to Search

No subscription, no login, no fee — ever. The public registry is a public good. Every Nigerian deserves the right to check before they transact, regardless of what they can afford.

2

We Will Be Transparent About What We Do Not Know

When we are not sure about a record, we say so. When timelines slip, we tell you. This roadmap will be updated whenever something changes — not buried and forgotten.

3

Every Record Is Treated With Legal Caution

We do not publish unverified accusations. Every record is reviewed before going live. We use community-reported and allegedly throughout our language. We have a dispute process. We are building something credible, not a mob platform.

4

Victim Privacy Is Protected Until You Choose Otherwise

If you report fraud to us, your identity is never published without your explicit consent. We protect our community of reporters the same way we protect the broader public.

5

We Will Grow With the Community, Not Ahead of It

Every major new feature — the submission portal, community corroboration, personal alerts — was shaped by questions and requests from real users. We build what you need, in the order you need it.

6

We Will Share Our Progress Openly

Every three months, we will publish a public update — how many records are live, how many searches were conducted, what new features launched, and what is delayed. No vanity metrics. Just honest progress.
